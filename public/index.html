Our approach

Not just here for the good times.

Plenty of providers will tell you why they're the best. We're not going to do that. We'll show up, do the work, and let you judge for yourself.

Real support means being there on the hard days too, not only the easy ones. We're realistic about what that looks like, and we'd rather be honest with you than polished at you.

We can't promise we're the best. We can promise we'll try our hardest, and that the care is real.

What we offer

Supports shaped around you

Everyday help delivered with respect and consistency. We support people living with physical disability, mental health conditions, and both together.

01

Daily living & personal care

Dignified, hands-on support with the routines of daily life, at your pace and on your terms.

02

Getting out & making friends

Socialising, meeting people and building a circle. We'll come along, help with the organising, and take the pressure out of showing up.

03

Hobbies & new interests

Trying something new is easier with company. We'll help you find it, book it, get there, and do it without the hassle of going it alone.

04

Cleaning & household tasks

Keeping your space comfortable and cared for, so home always feels like somewhere you can relax.

05

Community access

Appointments, shopping, work, study or a day out. Practical help to stay connected to the world around you.

06

Capacity building

Practical skill-building toward greater independence, from cooking and budgeting to navigating services.

07

Supported independent living

Reliable in-home support that helps you live more independently, with the right help always close by.

08

Transport & access

Getting where you need to be, safely, on time, and without the stress of working out how.

2 – 20

Hours a week. Either is fine.

Whether you need as little as two hours a week or as many as twenty, we'll work with you. The goal is to actually assist you in the way you need, not to fill a roster.

Help lines & resources

Support, whether or not you choose us

If you need to talk to someone right now, these services are free, confidential and staffed by trained people. You do not need to be an NDIS participant to call them.

000

In an emergency, or if life is in danger, call Triple Zero (000). Don't wait.

Lifeline

13 11 14
24 hours, 7 days

Crisis support and suicide prevention for anyone in Australia. You can also text 0477 13 11 14 or chat online.

lifeline.org.au

13YARN

13 92 76
24 hours, 7 days

Crisis support run by Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people, for mob. A culturally safe space to yarn, without judgement or shame.

13yarn.org.au

QLife

1800 184 527
3pm – 9pm, every day

Anonymous, free LGBTIQA+SB peer support and referral. Talk about sexuality, gender, bodies, feelings or relationships with someone from the community.

qlife.org.au

Beyond Blue

1300 22 4636
24 hours, 7 days

Free counselling and support for anxiety, depression and general mental health, by phone, webchat or email.

beyondblue.org.au

1800RESPECT

1800 737 732
24 hours, 7 days

National domestic, family and sexual violence counselling and support. Text 0458 737 732, or chat and video call online.

1800respect.org.au

Blue Knot Foundation

1300 657 380
9am – 5pm, 7 days

Counselling, information and referral for adult survivors of complex trauma, childhood abuse and violence, and for the people supporting them.

blueknot.org.au

Butterfly Foundation

1800 33 4673
8am – midnight, 7 days

Support for eating disorders and body image concerns, for you or someone you care about. This is a support line, not a crisis line. Closed on public holidays.

butterfly.org.au

Epilepsy Queensland

1300 852 853
Mon – Fri, business hours

Information, one-to-one support and seizure management planning for Queenslanders living with epilepsy and those who support them.

epilepsyqueensland.com.au

NDIS

1800 800 110
Mon – Fri, 8am – 8pm

The National Disability Insurance Agency, for questions about your plan, your funding, or how to access the scheme.

ndis.gov.au

Finding a counsellor

1800 022 222
24 hours, 7 days

healthdirect can help you find local health and mental health services, and talk through what kind of support might suit you. Your GP can also arrange a mental health care plan.

healthdirect.gov.au

These services are independent of Prismatic Care. Details were correct at the time of writing. Hours and phone numbers can change, so please check the service's own website if you're unsure.
